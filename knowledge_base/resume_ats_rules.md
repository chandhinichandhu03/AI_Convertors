# Applicant Tracking System (ATS) Resume Guidelines

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are software applications used by employers to manage the recruitment process, including screening, sorting, and ranking job applications. Following these rules ensures that resumes parse correctly.

## 1. Resume Formatting & Structure
* **File Types**: Resumes should be uploaded in PDF, DOCX, or plain text (TXT). PDF is preferred for visual integrity, while DOCX is highly compatible with older ATS engines.
* **Layouts**: Stick to a single-column layout. Multi-column tables, text boxes, and sidebars often cause text to be read out of order or ignored entirely.
* **Headers and Footers**: Avoid placing contact information inside headers or footers, as some ATS parsers do not scan these sections.
* **Fonts**: Use standard, web-safe fonts like Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, Garamond, or Georgia. Avoid decorative fonts. Size should range between 10pt and 12pt for body text, and 14pt to 16pt for headings.
* **Tables and Graphics**: Avoid icons, charts, progress bars, images, or nested tables. ATS cannot extract text from image graphics.

## 2. Mandatory Resume Sections
An ATS looks for specific section headings to parse experience, education, and skills. Use standard, clear headings:
* **Contact Information**: Full name, email address, phone number, location (City, State/Country), LinkedIn profile URL.
* **Professional Summary**: A 3-4 sentence elevator pitch containing core value, experience level, and key achievements.
* **Work Experience**: Job Title, Company Name, Location, Dates of Employment (Month & Year), and bullet points detailing accomplishments.
* **Skills**: A categorized list of hard and soft skills.
* **Education**: Degree name, Major, Institution, Graduation Year.
* **Projects / Certifications**: Project titles, summaries, and technical certifications.

## 3. Resume Length & White Space
* **Page Count**: 1 page for entry-level and up to 5 years of experience. Maximum of 2 pages for mid-to-senior level. 3 pages is only acceptable for executive-level, academic, or research CVs.
* **Margins**: Use standard 0.5 to 1-inch margins on all sides to maintain readability and scan compatibility.

## 4. Keyword Optimization & Density
* **Keyword Matching**: Tailor the resume to match the Job Description. The ATS ranks candidates based on the presence and frequency of exact keywords found in the JD.
* **Hard Skills**: List specific tools, technologies, methodologies, and frameworks (e.g., "React", "Python", "Docker", "Machine Learning", "Financial Analysis").
* **Soft Skills**: Include professional traits (e.g., "Project Management", "Leadership", "Cross-functional Collaboration", "Analytical Thinking").
* **Keyword Stuffing**: Do not repeat keywords unnecessarily or write hidden keywords in white text. The parser will detect duplicate keywords and flag the resume as spam.

## 5. Bullets and Action Verbs
* **Action-Oriented Language**: Start every bullet point with a strong action verb (e.g., "Led", "Developed", "Optimized", "Designed", "Initiated", "Streamlined", "Accelerated", "Implemented"). Avoid passive phrases like "Responsible for" or "Worked on".
* **Quantification / Metrics (STAR Method)**: Describe accomplishments using the **STAR** framework:
  * **S**ituation / **T**ask: The challenge or context.
  * **A**ction: What you did to solve it (using specific tools/technologies).
  * **R**esults: The quantifiable outcome (e.g., "improving page speed by 35%", "reducing server cost by $12,000 annually", "increasing team throughput by 15%").
* **Avoid Weak Bullet Points**:
  * *Weak*: "Worked on React website."
  * *Strong*: "Developed a responsive React web application utilizing state management, improving rendering speed by 35%."
