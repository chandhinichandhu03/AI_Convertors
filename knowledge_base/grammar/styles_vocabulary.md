# Writing Styles, Tone Mappings, and Vocabulary Enhancers

Guidelines for shifting writing styles, enhancing vocabulary choices, and applying reading complexity improvements.

## 1. Mappings of Tone & Style
* **Professional / Business**: Objective, clear, respectful. Uses active verbs, avoids slang and excessive punctuation. Example: "We look forward to receiving your proposal."
* **Academic**: Formal, passive voice when discussing research, precise citations, objective, complex sentence structure. Example: "The experiment yielded results indicating a significant shift."
* **Casual / Informal**: Personal pronouns, contractions allowed, conversational flow. Example: "Let me know what you think about this plan."
* **Technical**: Precise definitions, logical progression, lists, beginner-friendly instructions when explaining complex concepts.
* **Creative / Storytelling**: Descriptive adjectives, narrative arcs, figurative expressions, varied sentence lengths.
* **Persuasive / Marketing**: Focus on benefits, call-to-actions, direct addressing of the audience, emotive adjectives.

## 2. Vocabulary Improver & Word Variety
* **Weak Verbs**: Replace auxiliary constructs (e.g. "make a decision" -> "decide", "give an explanation" -> "explain").
* **Repeated Words**: Identify and replace frequent nouns/verbs with context-aware synonyms to improve sentence variety and readability score.
* **Advanced Vocabulary**: Elevate simple language to advanced words (e.g., "very big" -> "immense", "help" -> "facilitate", "show" -> "demonstrate").
* **Sentence Variety**: Alternate between short, punchy sentences and complex/compound structures to maintain reader engagement.
