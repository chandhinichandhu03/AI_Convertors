# Physics Equations, Units, and Dim Analysis

A reference for physical laws, formulas, derivations, SI units, and dimensional equations.

## 1. Classical Mechanics & Energy
* **Newton's Second Law**: $F = m \cdot a$
  * *SI Unit*: Newton ($N = kg \cdot m/s^2$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M L T^{-2}]$
  * *Derivation*: Acceleration is the second derivative of position, $a = d^2x/dt^2$. Force is defined as change in momentum over time, $F = dp/dt$.
* **Torque**: $\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$
  * *SI Unit*: Newton-meter ($N \cdot m$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M L^2 T^{-2}]$
* **Kinetic Energy**: $KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2$
  * *SI Unit*: Joule ($J = kg \cdot m^2/s^2$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M L^2 T^{-2}]$

## 2. Electromagnetism & Circuits
* **Ohm's Law**: $V = I \cdot R$ (Voltage = Current * Resistance)
  * *Voltage (V)*: Volt ($V = W/A$)
  * *Current (I)*: Ampere ($A$)
  * *Resistance (R)*: Ohm ($\Omega = V/A$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M L^2 T^{-3} I^{-1}]$
* **Capacitance**: $C = Q / V$
  * *SI Unit*: Farad ($F$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M^{-1} L^{-2} T^4 I^2]$

## 3. Thermodynamics & Wave Physics
* **Pressure**: $P = F / A$
  * *SI Unit*: Pascal ($Pa = N/m^2$)
  * *Dimensions*: $[M L^{-1} T^{-2}]$
* **Frequency & Waves**: $v = f \cdot \lambda$ (Velocity = Frequency * Wavelength)
  * *Frequency (f)*: Hertz ($Hz = 1/s$)
  * *Wavelength ($\lambda$)*: Meter ($m$)
* **Quantum Units**: Planck's relation $E = h \cdot f$, where $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} J \cdot s$).
