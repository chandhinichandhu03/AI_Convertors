# Verb Tenses, Voice Shifts, and Direct/Indirect Speech Mappings

A structural reference for identifying and translating active voice, passive voice, tense configurations, and reported speech.

## 1. Active vs. Passive Voice Mappings
* **Active Voice**: The subject performs the action (e.g. "The developer wrote the code").
  * *Structure*: Subject + Verb + Object.
* **Passive Voice**: The subject receives the action (e.g. "The code was written by the developer").
  * *Structure*: Object + auxiliary "to be" + past participle + "by" + Subject.
  * *Rules*: Shifts the focus of a sentence. Useful in academic or formal contexts where the actor is unknown or unimportant.

## 2. Verb Tenses Mappings
* **Simple**:
  * *Present*: `Subject + verb(s)` (e.g., "I code").
  * *Past*: `Subject + verb(ed)` (e.g., "I coded").
  * *Future*: `Subject + will + verb` (e.g., "I will code").
* **Continuous**:
  * *Present*: `am/is/are + verb(ing)`.
  * *Past*: `was/were + verb(ing)`.
  * *Future*: `will be + verb(ing)`.
* **Perfect**:
  * *Present*: `has/have + past participle`.
  * *Past*: `had + past participle`.
  * *Future*: `will have + past participle`.
* **Perfect Continuous**:
  * *Present*: `has/have + been + verb(ing)`.
  * *Past*: `had + been + verb(ing)`.
  * *Future*: `will have + been + verb(ing)`.

## 3. Reported / Indirect Speech
* **Direct Speech**: Quotes exact words (e.g. "I am busy," she said).
* **Indirect Speech**: Reports words without quotes (e.g., She said that she was busy).
  * *Backshift Rules*: Present simple becomes past simple; past simple becomes past perfect; "will" becomes "would".
